# Unexpected

## Use

Use unexpectedness to open a knowledge gap or challenge a false assumption.

## Patterns

- "The problem is not X. It is Y."
- "Most teams try A. The faster path is B."
- "The hidden cost is not the tool. It is the handoff."
- "You do not need more leads. You need fewer unqualified demos."

## Rules

- The twist must be true and relevant.
- Do not use shock for its own sake.
- Explain the gap immediately after opening it.
- Tie the surprise to proof, not just opinion.
